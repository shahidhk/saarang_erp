Greetings from the Saarang organizing committee! Thank you for registering for Saarang! We will be sending out updates regarding some of our new events over the next few days, and this mail is about our exciting new event called Young Reporter.Read on for more!  

Want to tell the nation what it really wants to know? Do you think you can cut the crap and make an impact? Have you always been fascinated by the world of reporting? Then here's your chance!

Media has a significant impact on our lives - be it the sensationalised Aarushi Talwar murder case or the Ashok Ganguly allegations. Media was meant to bring to the people a fair and just assessment of happenings across the globe. It has the power to influence policies, pressurize Government policies and start revolutions. But is this enormous power being used judiciously? 

Saarang in association with The Times of India present to you Young Reporters! A unique contest to test the reporter in you. Go out onto the LOC or into remote villages of Tamil Nadu to bring to the people THE scoop of the decade. In the most ethical way possible, of course!

Do you have it in you to be the next Arnab Goswami? Register and find out!

Rules for the event:1. Teams of 32. Topics shall be released one day before the event3. Topics may be fiction or non-fiction4. Adequate information in the form of case studies will be given to the contestants for their referral5. Contestants are required to report the topic like a news broadcast on television. There can be different ways this can be done – either one person at the news station as an anchor, the other as an analyst and the third on the field. This shouldn’t be confused with a skit, because emphasis is on the oratory talent of the contestants and the ability to construct a story from raw data handed to them and make reasonable conclusions.6. Time limit of 10 minutes7. Points are awarded for interpretation of facts, correlation with circumstances, style of speech and ability to hold attention8. No props/costumes allowed

Prize money of Rs 10,000 at stake! And the possibility of your name in the paper!
